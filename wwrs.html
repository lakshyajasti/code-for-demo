WWRS Information

Worldwide Warehouse Redistribution Services

Worldwide Warehouse Redistribution Services (WWRS) is a Tri-service program that facilitates the transfer of materiel acquired under the US Arms Export Control Act either by an FMS Case or DCS Purchase. WWRS accepts materiel back on behalf of the US Government in order to fill other FMS requisitions. Materiel transfers using the WWRS process as outlined in Section 21 (m) consist of two separate two-party transfers. The first two-party transfer is between the seller and the USG, and the second two-party transfer is between the USG and the purchaser. The WWRS Program Management office is managed at Wright Patterson AFB, Ohio.

WWRS Goals

  • Reduce FMS customers' excess inventories.
  • Provide access to materiel at reduced cost.
  • Reduce lead times through redistribution of on-the-shelf assets instead of new procurement.
  • Enable purchase of needed FMS assets by materiel sellers with the proceeds or withdraw proceeds to their national treasury.

WWRS Benefits

  • Improve FMS customer support by reducing lead times and materiel costs associated with new procurement and stock fund surcharges.
  • Funds provided to sellers may be reinvested in the FMS program.
  • Reduce seller excess materiel storage costs.

Welcome to WWRS

WWRS redistributes serviceable, fully functioning spare parts and support equipment, previously purchased under the Arms Export Control Act, as a source for filling Foreign Military Sales (FMS) requisitions. USG requisitions may be filled upon receipt of a funded MIPR.

WWRS improves FMS customer support by reducing lead times and materiel costs associated with new procurement and stock fund surcharges, as well as the seller's FMS excess materiel storage costs. Funds provided to sellers may be reinvested in the FMS program. AFLCMC encourages the FMS community to exercise WWRS as an alternate support mechanism.

Program Overview

The success of the WWRS program is mutually dependent on meeting the sellers' and purchasers' requirements and the PMO's ability to facilitate global redistribution transactions.

WWRS aligns incoming FMS requisitions with worldwide excess serviceable materiel inventories to improve FMS requisition support. Materiel remains in the possession of the seller until the WWRS Program Management Office (PMO) requests the materiel. WWRS materiel is inspected, sanitized, documented, and repackaged at a USG contracted In-transit Inspection Point prior to its transfer to the purchaser. The USG takes title to the materiel prior to transfer, thereby maintaining anonymity of the FMS parties. Materiel failing visual inspection is returned to the selling country at the seller's expense. The seller has first option to replace the defective materiel with serviceable materiel prior to the selection of an alternate seller. All materiel shipments that pass inspection are subject to normal FMS Supply Discrepancy Report (SDR) criteria.

Virtual Warehouse and AFSAC Online

The WWRS is a virtual materiel warehouse, which lists excess, serviceable materiel on the WWRS web page by NSN, noun, ERRC, shelf life, annual demand, quantity, price, and end item(s). The DoD catalog price and source of supply are provided for comparative purposes. Interested purchasers may browse or download the entire inventory using the Download records button on the WWRS Home Page. WWRS applications, which include specific item searches, is provided via AFSAC Online (although an AFSAC Online password is not required).

The seller with the lowest priced NSN listed is selected to fill the requisition. When an insufficient quantity of the lowest priced NSN to fill the requisition exists, the next higher priced materiel is selected. This selection process is repeated until the requisition is filled or all WWRS inventory is depleted. Any unfilled portion of a requisition is canceled.

WWRS is hosted, in part, on AFSAC Online. While briefings and certain information is available to the public, most applications that support WWRS require an AFSAC Online userid and password. AFSAC Online supports more than just WWRS; it supports all Air Force FMS.

Buyer Information

All requisitions are filled in the order of receipt. WWRS requisitions with RIC 'FWW' are treated as "fill or kill" requisitions. If the desired WWRS materiel is depleted, the requisition is canceled. All other requisitions received with RIC 'FNH' will be passed on to another DoD source of supply, if WWRS cannot fill or partially fill the order. Status and bill lines are provided to CaseTRAC.

Two Methods of Purchasing WWRS Materiel

  • Materiel may be ordered by submitting a nonrecurring requisition with the Routing Identifier Code (RIC) of 'FWW' to CaseTRAC against the appropriate blanket order FMS requisition case. Requisitions that contain a NMCS indicator or advice codes BV, 6P, 6V, or 6W are rejected with an F6 rejection code.
  • USAF FMS requisitions submitted to CaseTRAC with the RIC of 'FNH' are automatically eligible for fill action by WWRS when the requested NSN is not currently on the purchaser's CLSSA case, retention quantities are zero or unavailable at ALC, demand code is nonrecurring, and advice codes BW/6P/6V/6W are not present.

Materiel Requisitioning

WWRS materiel may be purchased via CaseTRAC, Army CISIL, or Navy MISIL systems. Purchasers submit requisitions using MILSTRIP and may force route requisitions to WWRS via RIC 'FWW'. Status, pricing, and shipping information is available through these systems, STARR-PC, AFSAC Online, and PMO reports.

MISIL customers continue to submit requisitions using RIC 'FWW'. CISIL customers continue to submit requisitions using RIC 'FWW'. For status questions, contact the WWRS PMO.

Navy input questions may be directed to barry.l.magagna@navy.mil. Army inquiries may be directed to benjamin.d.rutz.civ@army.mil. USG purchasers may use funding documents such as a MIPR or MORD.

Seller Information

FMS customers who want to become materiel sellers should submit a Letter of Request (LOR), along with a data set of excess serviceable materiel. The required format is in Section III of the WWRS Customer Handbook. In addition to NSN, sellers provide quantity, desired unit price in US dollars and cents, and a unique alphanumeric ID code.

Sellers are required to have Internet access and request an AFSAC Online password to view WWRS web pages. Internet access should include e-mail capability for communications with the WWRS PMO. Potential purchasers may browse listings but do not see seller identity.

Seller Order Processing

Upon AFLCMC receipt of a requisition for WWRS-listed materiel, the selected seller is sent a Redistribution Order (RDO) with NSN, quantity, and extended dollar value. The RDO is authority for shipment to the In-Transit Inspection Point after seller acknowledgement and shipping data back to PMO.

Financial

Sellers pay a 10% redistribution fee (minimum $200) per RDO for materiel received at the In-Transit Inspection Point. Fees for seller-arranged lot transfers of at least 15 line items are discounted an additional 10% or 20%, depending on lot value.

The seller WWRS case is ordinarily written for approximately 10% of estimated value of materiel for sale and operates with terms of sale "Cash with Acceptance, Balance as Billed." Sellers eligible for Defense Transportation Services may request a transportation line be added to the WWRS case.
