AFSAC Applications

Select an application to manage requisitions, logistics, Case data, discrepancy reporting, and program metrics.

Contains tools for submitting Requisition Inputs for Foreign Military Sales programs, including both standard and non-standard requests. Provides a structured interface for submitting, tracking, and managing material and service requests.

Open Tool →

Logistics applications provide detailed insight into the requisitioning process, including catalog information and narratives. Enables customers to track the status and details of their full logistics pipeline.

Open Tool →

Case applications provide tools for enhanced FMS Case data reporting. Enables detailed tracking of case funding, obligations, expenditures, and overall case status across country programs.

Open Tool →

Supply Discrepancy Report (SDR) tools are used to report shipping or packaging discrepancies attributed to the responsibility of the shipper, and provide appropriate official responses and resolution tracking.

Open Tool →

The Cooperative Logistics Supply Support Arrangement (CLSSA) program module provides a dedicated interface for managing cooperative logistics arrangements between partner nations and the U.S. Air Force.

Open Tool →

PROS provides procurement and maintenance support for nonstandard and difficult-to-support standard items. Also serves as a contracting vehicle for specialized technical services through task orders.

Open Tool →

World Wide Warehouse Redistribution Services (WWRS) accepts materiel back on behalf of the U.S. Government in order to fill other FMS requisitions. Supports efficient redistribution of surplus defense materiel globally.

Open Tool →

The AFSAC Metrics tool automates production of regularly scheduled performance metrics and provides quick access to program data. Ideal for executive dashboards, trend analysis, and standard reporting packages.

Open Tool →
Access Required: All AFSAC Online tools require authorized credentials. If you do not have access, contact your country's Foreign Liaison Office or submit a request through our Contact page.
